Reading the Trends

✅ Consistent Strengths

Y5 Numeracy — Consistent Upward Trend
65.5% → 76.4% → 80.0%. The only domain showing unbroken improvement across all three years at Year 5.
Y5 Reading — Sustained High Performance
Has not dropped below 67.8% in three years. 2026 at 78.2% remains very strong.
Y3 Spelling — Rock Solid
64–67% Strong+ across all three years. Most cohort-independent result — suggests consistent delivery.
Y3 Numeracy Recovery
A dip in 2025 (52.4%) has bounced back to 70.4% in 2026 — likely a cohort effect, not systemic.

⚠️ Leadership Attention Required

Y5 Grammar — 2026 Dip
67.8% → 77.6% → 60.0%. Most likely cohort-driven: this group achieved only 41% Strong+ in Grammar as Y3s in 2024.
Y3 Grammar — Variable Across Cohorts
41.0% → 50.8% → 45.3%. No consistent upward trend. Grammar remains the weakest Y3 domain.
Y3 Reading — Plateaued
58.1% → 50.8% → 51.9%. Has not recovered to 2024 level. Worth monitoring.
Y5 Spelling Volatility
74.6% → 65.5% → 78.2%. Large swings suggest cohort-driven variation.
📌 Important Context: Cohort vs School Effects
Year-on-year variation in NAPLAN results is often driven by cohort characteristics rather than changes in teaching quality. The 2026 Y5 Grammar dip is the clearest example: this same cohort achieved only 41% Strong+ in Grammar as Year 3s in 2024 — so the 2026 result (60%) actually represents significant improvement for those students, even though the school-level number appears lower than 2025. Always read trends alongside the longitudinal student data.
